# Medieval Banquet

This feast is a three-course medieval-inspired meal built around rustic peasant comfort, a rich savory centerpiece, and a sweet finish. The menu features rustic pottage with trenchers, an Ember Day Tart with garlic butter mushrooms, and a medieval apple tart served with gyngerbrede and honey mead.

## Timeline

- The day before: Make the gyngerbrede and leave it at room temperature.
- 2 hours before dinner: Bake the apple tart and let it cool.
- 1 hour before dinner: Bake the Ember Day Tart.
- 30 minutes before dinner: Start the pottage and mushrooms.
- Feast time: Assemble the trenchers and serve the meal.

## Course 1:  The Peasants' Welcome

### Rustic Pottage

Historical Basis: The concept of using thick, day-old loaves of grain bread as edible plates (trenchers) to capture slow-simmered, vegetable-and-legume stews comes directly from historical accounts of lower-class dining across Europe. The base recipe mirrors traditional methods found in [Medieval Cookery](http://www.medievalcookery.com/recipes/gyngerbrede.html) databases and historic culinary documentation tracking 14th-century peasant staples.

#### Ingredients

| Serving | Ingredient | Notes |
| - | - | - |
| 3 cans | Brown lentils | Drained |
| 4 | Carrots | Chopped |
| 2 | Leeks | Chopped |
| 4 cups | Vegetable broth | |
| To taste | Salt | |
| To taste | Black pepper | |
| Pinch | Ground cumin | |

#### Instructions

1. Combine the lentils, carrots, leeks, and vegetable broth in a pot.
1. Season with salt, black pepper, and cumin.
1. Simmer gently over low heat for 20 minutes, until the vegetables are tender.
1. Watch closely so the broth does not evaporate completely.

### Trenchers & Honey Butter

Historical Basis: The concept of using thick, day-old loaves of grain bread as edible plates (trenchers) to capture slow-simmered, vegetable-and-legume stews comes directly from historical accounts of lower-class dining across Europe. The base recipe mirrors traditional methods found in [Medieval Cookery](http://www.medievalcookery.com/recipes/gyngerbrede.html) databases and historic culinary documentation tracking 14th-century peasant staples.

#### Ingredients

| Serving | Ingredient | Notes |
| - | - | - |
| 2 round loaves | Sourdough bread | Thickly sliced vertically |
| 1 stick | Unsalted butter | Softened |
| 3 tbsp | Honey | |

#### Instructions

1. Mix the butter and honey together.
1. Slice the sourdough into thick vertical slices.
1. Spread the honey butter on the bread and top with hot pottage before serving.

## Course 2: The Lord's Board

### Ember Day Tart

Historical Basis: This cheese-and-herb custard pie is inspired directly by "Tart de Bry" (a traditional medieval brie and saffron tart) and early English "Ember Day" fasting menus recorded in historical recipe books like The Forme of Cury (c. 1390), as referenced in [The Saffron Cheese and Honey Tart](https://app.ckbk.com/recipe/thes59695c10s001r009/saffron-cheese-and-honey-tart).

#### Ingredients

| Serving | Ingredient | Notes |
| - | - | - |
| 1 | Pie crust | Store-bought, pressed into a pie pan |
| 6 | Eggs | |
| 1 cup | Heavy cream | |
| 2 cups | Spinach | Chopped |
| 1 cup | Swiss cheese | Shredded |
| To taste | Salt | |
| Pinch | Ground nutmeg | |

#### Instructions

1. Press the pie crust into a pie pan and prick the bottom with a fork.
1. Blind-bake at 375°F for 10 minutes until lightly set.
1. Whisk together the eggs, cream, spinach, cheese, salt, and nutmeg.
1. Pour into the pre-baked crust and bake at 375°F for 35 minutes, until set.

### Garlic Butter Mushrooms

Historical Basis: "Funges" is the Middle English word for mushrooms. The simple technique of sautéing mushrooms in butter, leeks, or light wine is preserved in early English cooking scrolls from the late 14th century, which often called for wild-foraged mushrooms to be boiled or fried gently to accompany wealthier tables.

#### Ingredients

| Serving | Ingredient | Notes |
| - | - | - |
| 2 lb | Cremini mushrooms | Sliced |
| 4 tbsp | Butter | |
| 4 cloves | Garlic | Minced |
| 1/2 cup | Dry white wine | |
| To taste | Parsley | Chopped |

#### Instructions

1. Sauté the mushrooms in butter with garlic until browned.
1. Add the white wine and reduce for 3 minutes.
1. Toss with parsley before serving.

## Course 3: The Grand Finale

### Medieval Apple Tart

Historical Basis: Spicing standard local orchard fruits with ginger and cinnamon—rather than using heavy sugar—was a classic hallmark of aristocratic medieval desserts. A highly similar variant known as "Tart de Brymlent" appears in historical archives detailing sweet-savory fruit pastries, as seen in [Tart de Brymlent](https://recipeland.com/recipe/v/tart-de-brymlent-a-medieval-len-45188).

#### Ingredients

| Serving | Ingredient | Notes |
| - | - | - |
| 6 | Baking apples | Peeled and sliced |
| 1/4 cup | Sugar | |
| 1 tsp | Ground cinnamon | |
| 1/4 tsp | Ground ginger | |
| 2 | Pie crusts | Store-bought |

#### Instructions

1. Toss the apples with sugar, cinnamon, and ginger.
1. Place one pie crust in a pie dish and fill with the apples.
1. Top with the second pie crust, crimp the edges, and cut vents in the top.
1. Bake at 375°F for 45 minutes, until golden.

### Medieval Gyngerbrede

Historical Basis: This is an exact conceptual adaptation of a 15th-century recipe found in the Harleian Manuscript 279 (c. 1430). The original text specifically instructs the cook to boil honey, skim it clean, mix in powdered pepper and ginger, and bind it into a sliceable paste using "grated bread," as described in [Medieval Gyngerbrede](https://historydollop.com/2018/12/11/medieval-gyngerbrede/), [Gode Cookery](http://www.godecookery.com/goderec/grec42.htm), and [Medieval Recipes: Ginger Bread](https://medievalbritain.com/type/medieval-life/medieval-recipes/medieval-recipes-ginger-bread/).

#### Ingredients

| Serving | Ingredient | Notes |
| - | - | - |
| 1 cup | Honey | |
| 1 tsp | Ground ginger | |
| 1/2 tsp | Ground cinnamon | |
| Pinch | Black pepper | |
| 2.5 cups | Plain breadcrumbs | Dry, unseasoned |

#### Instructions

1. Bring the honey to a boil and skim off any foam.
1. Stir in the ginger, cinnamon, and black pepper.
1. Remove from the heat and stir in the breadcrumbs until a thick dough forms.
1. Press into a baking dish, cool completely, and slice into diamond shapes.

## Libations

### Honey Mead

Historical Basis: Archaeological and historical records place honey mead as one of the oldest fermented beverages in human history. It was widely consumed throughout the Middle Ages as an alternative to water and unsafe fresh milk, making it a prominent centerpiece of historical beverage logs.

Serve chilled or at room temperature with the meal.
